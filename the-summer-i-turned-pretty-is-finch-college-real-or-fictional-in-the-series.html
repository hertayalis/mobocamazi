    Exploring the Academic Paths in Prime Video’s Hit Series

    “The Summer I Turned Pretty,” a captivating series on Prime Video, presents its leading characters at a pivotal moment in their lives – the transition to college. The series, starring Lola Tung as Belly Conklin, Christopher Briney as Conrad Fisher, and Gavin Casalegno as Jeremiah Fisher, delves into the complexities of young adulthood, including crucial decisions about higher education.

    The Intrigue of Finch College: Fact or Fiction?

    A significant point of discussion in the series is the potential college choice of Belly and Jeremiah, with Finch College frequently mentioned. But is Finch College an actual institution? In the realm of “The Summer I Turned Pretty,” Finch College is a fictional creation for the show. However, historically, a real Finch College did exist in Manhattan, New York City, from 1900 to 1976, exclusively for women’s undergraduate education.

    In the show’s context, Finch College is the prospective choice for Jeremiah, and Belly also shows interest in attending. The unfolding of Season 2 centers around their decisions regarding this fictional college.

    Conrad Fisher’s Real-World Dilemma

    Contrasting the fictional Finch College, Conrad Fisher’s narrative intertwines with real-world renowned universities. He is initially enrolled at Brown University in Providence, Rhode Island, but contemplates a transfer to Stanford University in California, a dream shared by him and his late mother, Susannah. This plotline adds a layer of reality to the series, resonating with viewers familiar with these prestigious institutions.

    The storyline intricately balances the fictional and real elements of college life, reflecting the broader themes of young love, family dynamics, and the journey of self-discovery that “The Summer I Turned Pretty” so vividly portrays.

    “The Summer I Turned Pretty” not only captures the essence of teenage romance and family drama but also navigates the complex decisions surrounding college choices, blending both fictional and real-life elements.
